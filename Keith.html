Background

I am a Civil Engineer by training and an infrastructure strategist by practice. With a Master of Science in Transportation Engineering from Mapua University and research experience at UC Berkeley's Institute for Transportation Studies, I bridge rigorous technical analysis with strategic commercial thinking.

"I work at the intersection of policy, finance, and engineering — where the decisions that shape a nation's mobility are made."

Currently serving as an Infrastructure Consultant at the Asian Development Bank, I advise on landmark PPP transactions across the Philippines and the broader Southeast Asia region — from the award-winning NAIA PPP to ongoing concession advisory for flagship metro and commuter rail systems.

My work spans the full project lifecycle: from origination and feasibility, through transaction structuring and commercial close, to policy reform and institutional capacity building. I bring both government and multilateral perspectives, having served previously as Engineer III/IV at the Department of Transportation.
